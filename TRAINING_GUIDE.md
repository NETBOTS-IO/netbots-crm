## 1. What is a CRM?

CRM stands for **Customer Relationship Management**. Think of it like a smart notebook where we keep track of every business we want to work with.

Instead of writing names and phone numbers in a diary or Excel sheet, we use this CRM software to:

- **Save** information about businesses (leads)
- **Track** where each lead is in our sales process
- **Assign** team members to each lead
- **See** who is performing well and who needs help
- **Calculate** how much commission each person earns

**Simple analogy:** Imagine you are fishing. The CRM is your fishing log — it tells you where each fish is, who threw the bait, who is reeling it in, and when the fish was caught.

## 2. What is a Lead?

A **Lead** is a potential customer — a business that might need our services but has NOT paid us yet.

### Types of Leads (Temperature)

| Temperature | What It Means | Example |
|-------------|--------------|---------|
| **Cold** 🥶 | We found the business but they don't know about us yet | A restaurant we found on Google Maps |
| **Warm** 🌤️ | They have shown some interest (replied, asked questions) | A hotel owner who replied to our WhatsApp message |
| **SQL** 🔥 | They are seriously interested and we are discussing price/deal | A retail shop owner who said "Send me the proposal" |
| **Closed** ✅ | They said YES, signed up, and are now a paying customer | A pharmacy that paid for SEO service |

### Lead Stages (Pipeline)

The "pipeline" is like a journey. Every lead moves through these steps:

1. **Identify** → We found the business (scraped data, Google Maps, etc.)
2. **Qualify** → We checked if they are a real, active business worth contacting
3. **Nurture** → We are talking to them, building trust, sharing information
4. **Close** → They have agreed to the deal (price, service, timeline decided)
5. **Onboard** → Contract signed, payment done, work has started
6. **Retain** → They are a happy active client, we keep them satisfied
7. **Refer** → They are so happy that they are sending us new customers

## 3. What is a Client?

A **Client** is a lead that has been successfully **converted** — meaning they agreed to our service, paid money, and now we are working for them.

**Lead → Client**  
(A lead becomes a client when the deal is closed and payment is received)

### Client Deal Types

| Deal Type | What It Means |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Monthly Subscription** | Client pays us every month (e.g., ₨25,000/month for SEO) |
| **One Time** | Client pays once for a single project (e.g., ₨50,000 for a website) |
| **Weekly** | Client pays every week |
| **Annual** | Client pays once a year for the full year |
| **Lifetime Deal** | Client pays once and gets service forever |
| **Enterprise** | Big custom deal, usually for large companies |

## 4. How The Whole Process Works (End-to-End)

Here is the complete flow from start to finish:

```
Step 1: LEAD COLLECTOR scrapes/finds business data
           ↓
Step 2: LEAD COLLECTOR adds business to CRM as a new lead (Stage: Identify)
           ↓
Step 3: LEAD VERIFIER reviews and checks the data quality
           ↓
Step 4: LEAD VERIFIER marks it as verified (Stage moves to: Qualify)
           ↓
Step 5: LEAD CLOSER contacts the business (call / WhatsApp / email)
           ↓
Step 6: LEAD CLOSER nurtures the relationship (Stage: Nurture)
           ↓
Step 7: LEAD CLOSER negotiates price and service (Stage: Close)
           ↓
Step 8: Deal is finalized → Lead is CONVERTED to CLIENT (Stage: Onboard)
           ↓
Step 9: Fulfillment team starts delivering the service
           ↓
Step 10: COMMISSIONS are automatically generated for the whole team
```

### Commission Split Rules

The total commission for converting one lead into a client is split among 3 roles and **cannot exceed 15%** of the deal value:

| Role | Maximum Commission | Example (on ₨100,000 deal) |
|------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Lead Collector | Up to 2% | Up to ₨2,000 |
| Lead Verifier | Up to 5% | Up to ₨5,000 |
| Lead Closer | Up to 10% | Up to ₨10,000 |
| **TOTAL** | **Max 15%** | **Max ₨15,000** |

## 5. Our Services (What We Sell)

Here is a detailed breakdown of every service we offer. Understanding these is **critical** for both verification and sales.

### 5.1 Google Business Profile SEO (GBP SEO)

**What it is:** Optimizing a business's Google Maps listing so it shows up when people search nearby. For example, when someone types "best restaurant near me", we make sure our client's business appears at the top.

**Who needs it:** Any local business — restaurants, pharmacies, hotels, retail shops, clinics, salons.

**What we do:**
- Optimize the business name, description, and categories
- Add high-quality photos
- Get real customer reviews
- Post updates and offers regularly
- Add correct business hours, address, phone number
- Fix NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency across the internet

### 5.2 Website SEO

**What it is:** Making a business's website appear higher in Google search results.

**Who needs it:** Any business that has a website and wants more visitors from Google.

**What we do:**
- Keyword research (find what people are searching for)
- On-page SEO (fix titles, descriptions, headings, content)
- Off-page SEO (get backlinks from other websites)
- Technical SEO (fix website speed, mobile-friendliness, errors)
- Content creation (write blog posts and articles)

### 5.3 Social Media Management & Marketing (SMM)

**What it is:** Managing a business's Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and other social media accounts.

**Who needs it:** Any business that wants to grow their online presence and connect with customers.

**What we do:**
- Create and schedule posts
- Design graphics and visuals
- Run paid ad campaigns (Facebook Ads, Instagram Ads)
- Respond to comments and messages
- Grow followers organically

### 5.4 Graphic Designing

**What it is:** Creating visual content like logos, banners, brochures, social media posts, and marketing materials.

**Who needs it:** Any business that needs professional-looking visuals.

### 5.5 Website Development

**What it is:** Building a new website from scratch or redesigning an existing one.

**Who needs it:** Businesses that have no website, or their current website looks outdated/broken.

### 5.6 Custom Software Development

**What it is:** Building custom tools, apps, or internal systems for businesses.

**Who needs it:** Businesses with unique needs that off-the-shelf software cannot solve.

### 5.7 SaaS Products

**What it is:** Selling subscriptions to our own ready-made software tools.

**Who needs it:** Businesses looking for ready-to-use digital solutions.

## 6. Role 1: Lead Collector — Full Guide

### Your Job in One Line
**Find businesses that might need our services and add them to the CRM.**

### Where to Find Leads

1. **Google Maps Scraping** — Use our data scraper tool to extract business listings from Google Maps
2. **Manual Google Maps Search** — Search for specific industries in specific cities
3. **Social Media** — Browse Facebook pages, Instagram business profiles, LinkedIn company pages
4. **Business Directories** — Yelp, Yellow Pages, local directories
5. **Referrals** — Ask existing clients or friends for introductions

### What Information to Collect

When adding a lead to the CRM, fill in as many fields as possible:

| Field | Required? | Where to Find It |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Company Name** | ✅ YES | Google Maps, Website, Facebook Page |
| **Contact Name** | Best effort | Website "About" or "Contact" page |
| **Phone** | ✅ YES | Google Maps listing, Website, Facebook |
| **Email** | Best effort | Website "Contact" page, Facebook "About" section |
| **Website** | Best effort | Google Maps listing |
| **Address** | Best effort | Google Maps listing |
| **Business Type** | ✅ YES | Select the correct category (pharmacy, hotel, restaurant, etc.) |
| **Category** | Best effort | Google Maps category (e.g., "Italian Restaurant") |
| **Review Count** | Best effort | Google Maps listing |
| **Average Rating** | Best effort | Google Maps listing |
| **Instagram** | Best effort | Website footer, Google search "[business name] Instagram" |
| **Facebook** | Best effort | Google search "[business name] Facebook" |

### Step-by-Step: How to Add a Lead

1. Log into the CRM
2. Go to **Leads Pipeline** from the sidebar
3. Click **"+ Add Lead"** button (top right)
4. Fill in the form with as much detail as possible
5. Set **Stage** to "Identify" (this is the default)
6. Set **Temperature** to "Cold" (this is the default)
7. Set **Priority** to "Medium" (unless the lead looks very promising)
8. Click **Save**

### Quality Tips

- **DO NOT** add incomplete data. At minimum, you need: Company Name + Phone Number + Business Type
- **DO NOT** add the same business twice. Search the CRM first!
- **DO** add the Google Maps review count and rating — the verifier needs this
- **DO** include the website URL if they have one
- **DO** note social media links — this helps the verifier decide which service to pitch

## 7. Role 2: Lead Verifier — Full Guide

### Your Job in One Line
**Check every lead's data for accuracy AND evaluate the business to decide which service to pitch.**

This is the most critical role in the pipeline. A badly verified lead wastes everyone's time. A well-verified lead with the right service recommendation makes the closer's job 10x easier.

### PART A: Data Verification Checklist

Go through every lead one by one and verify these fields:

#### ✅ Checkpoint 1: Basic Contact Info

| Check | How to Verify | Pass / Fail Criteria |
|-------|--------------|---------------------|
| Company Name | Search on Google | Must match a real, active business |
| Phone Number | Call it or search on Truecaller | Must be a working number that someone answers |
| Email | Check format, try basic search | Must be a real email (not "info@example.com" placeholder) |
| Address | Google Maps verification | Must match the actual business location |

#### ✅ Checkpoint 2: Google Business Profile Evaluation

Open the business on Google Maps and check:

| What to Look At | What It Tells You | Good / Bad Sign |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Review Count** | How popular the business is | ✅ Good: 50+ reviews. ⚠️ Needs help: Under 20 reviews |
| **Average Rating** | Customer satisfaction | ✅ Good: 4.0+. ⚠️ Needs work: Under 3.5 |
| **Photos** | How professional their listing looks | ✅ Good: 10+ quality photos. ❌ Bad: 0-3 blurry photos |
| **Business Hours** | Are they active? | ✅ Good: Complete hours listed. ❌ Bad: "Hours not available" |
| **Description** | Is their profile optimized? | ✅ Good: Detailed description with keywords. ❌ Bad: Empty or one-liner |
| **Category** | Is it set correctly? | ✅ Good: Specific category (e.g., "Italian Restaurant"). ❌ Bad: Generic (e.g., "Business") |
| **Posts** | Do they post updates? | ✅ Good: Recent posts (last 30 days). ❌ Bad: No posts ever |
| **Q&A** | Are customer questions answered? | ✅ Good: Owner replies. ❌ Bad: No answers |
| **Booking/Menu/Products** | Are they using Google's features? | ✅ Good: These features are set up. ❌ Bad: Not using them |

**🎯 DECISION:** If the Google Business Profile is weak (few reviews, no photos, no description), this business is a **strong candidate** for our **Google Business SEO** service!

#### ✅ Checkpoint 3: Website Evaluation

Open the business website and check:

| What to Look At | What It Tells You | Good / Bad Sign |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Does it exist?** | Do they have a website at all? | ❌ No website = pitch **Website Development** |
| **Mobile friendly?** | Open on phone — does it look good? | ❌ Broken on mobile = pitch **Website Development** |
| **Loading speed** | Does it load in under 3 seconds? | ❌ Slow = needs **Website SEO** (technical) |
| **SSL (https://)** | Is the site secure? | ❌ No SSL = needs fixing |
| **Design quality** | Does it look modern and professional? | ❌ Looks old/ugly = pitch **Website Development** |
| **Content quality** | Is the text well-written? Are there blog posts? | ❌ Thin content = needs **Website SEO** |
| **Google ranking** | Search their business name — where do they appear? | ❌ Not on first page = needs **Website SEO** |
| **Contact page** | Do they have a working contact form? | ❌ Missing = needs improvement |

**Quick Google Test:** Search for their main keyword (e.g., "best pharmacy in Lahore"). If they are NOT on the first page, they need **Website SEO**.

#### ✅ Checkpoint 4: Social Media Evaluation

Check their presence on Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.:

| What to Look At | What It Tells You | Good / Bad Sign |
|-----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Do they have accounts?** | Basic online presence | ❌ No accounts = pitch **SMM** |
| **Last post date** | Are they active? | ❌ No posts in 3+ months = pitch **SMM** |
| **Post quality** | Professional or amateur? | ❌ Low-quality images/text = pitch **Graphic Designing** + **SMM** |
| **Follower count** | Audience size | ⚠️ Very few followers = needs growth |
| **Engagement** | Likes, comments, shares on posts | ❌ Zero engagement = their current strategy is not working |
| **Running ads?** | Check Facebook Ad Library | If running ads but poorly = they need our help |

### PART B: Service Recommendation Matrix

Based on your evaluation, recommend the **target service** in the CRM:

| Situation Found | Recommended Service |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| No website at all | **Website Development** |
| Website exists but looks old / broken on mobile | **Website Development** |
| Website exists but not ranking on Google | **Website SEO** |
| Google Business Profile is weak / few reviews | **Google Business SEO** |
| No social media accounts OR dead accounts | **Social Media Management & Marketing** |
| Social media exists but posts look amateur | **Graphic Designing** + **SMM** |
| Business needs a custom app or tool | **Software Development** |
| Business needs a logo or brand identity | **Graphic Designing** |

### PART C: How to Mark a Lead as Verified in CRM

1. Open the lead in the CRM
2. Review all the data (correct any errors you find)
3. Set the **Target Service** dropdown to your recommended service
4. Add your notes in the **Notes** field explaining what you found
5. Move the **Stage** from "Identify" to **"Qualify"**
6. Set **Priority** based on how likely they are to buy:
   - **Urgent** — They clearly need help AND have money (big business, bad online presence)
   - **High** — They need help and seem like a good fit
   - **Medium** — Standard prospect
   - **Low** — Might not have budget or might not be interested

### PART D: Red Flags (Mark Lead as Low Priority or Delete)

- Business is permanently closed
- Phone number is disconnected / wrong
- The business is very small (1-person operation with no revenue)
- Business is in a country/region we don't serve
- Duplicate entry (already in the CRM)

## 8. Role 3: Lead Closer — Full Guide

### Your Job in One Line
**Contact verified leads, build relationships, negotiate deals, and close sales.**

### PART A: Before You Contact

Before calling or messaging a lead, always:

1. **Read the verifier's notes** — Know what service was recommended and why
2. **Check the Google Business Profile** yourself — See the business with your own eyes
3. **Check their website and social media** — Understand their current situation
4. **Prepare your pitch** based on the recommended target service

### PART E: Closing the Deal in CRM

When a customer agrees:

1. Go to the Lead in the CRM
2. Click **"Convert to Client"** button
3. Fill in the conversion form:
   - **Deal Type** — Monthly Subscription, One Time, etc.
   - **Plan Type** — Starter, Growth, Pro, etc.
   - **Deal Amount** — Total agreed amount
   - **Upfront Paid** — How much they paid now
   - **Start Date** — When the service begins
   - **Engaged Team** — Add yourself and others who worked on this lead, with commission amounts
4. Click **Save** — The lead will automatically move to the **Clients** section

## 9. CRM Page-by-Page Guide

### Dashboard
- Shows overall stats: total leads, total clients, revenue, and conversion rates
- Quick overview of team performance
- **Who can see:** Everyone with `view_dashboard` permission

### Leads Pipeline
- Main page to view, filter, search, and manage all leads
- Use filters at the top to narrow down by Stage, Temperature, Priority, or Service
- Click on any lead card to see full details
- **Who can see:** Everyone with `can_view_leads` permission
- **Who can add:** Only `can_add_leads` permission (Lead Collectors)
- **Who can edit:** Only `can_edit_leads` permission (Verifiers, Closers)

### Lead Details
- Full view of a single lead with all information
- View and edit contact info, business details, social media links
- Add notes and internal notes
- Book demos and track demo history
- Convert the lead to a client when the deal closes

### Clients
- Directory of all converted (paying) clients
- View active subscriptions, payment details, and contract dates
- Track churn risk and renewal dates
- **Who can see:** Only `manage_clients` permission (Closers, Admin)

### Performance Stats
- Individual KPI tracking (leads submitted, verified, closed)
- Points and ranking system
- Conversion rate metrics

### Team Management
- Add new team members (Admin only)
- Assign roles and designations
- View team directory

### Permissions
- Granular access control for each team member
- Apply preset templates (Supervisor, Collector, Verifier, Closer)
- Toggle individual permissions ON/OFF
- **Admin only**

### Commissions
- View generated commission records
- Track pending and paid commissions
- Export to PDF

### Payouts
- Log batch payouts to team members
- Track payout history
- **Admin only**

### Leaderboard
- Gamified ranking of team members
- Based on points earned from leads submitted, verified, and closed
- Updated in real-time

### Audit Logs
- Track every action performed in the CRM
- Who clicked what, when, and on which page
- **Admin only**

### Help & Docs
- System documentation (this guide, summarized)
- Definitions of all dropdowns, fields, and options
- Accessible to everyone

## 10. Glossary of Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|

| **Client** | A customer who has paid and is receiving our service |

| **Temperature** | How interested/ready the lead is (Cold → Warm → SQL → Closed) |
| **SQL** | Sales Qualified Lead — they are seriously interested |
| **Conversion** | When a lead becomes a paying client |
| **Commission** | The percentage of the deal that the team member earns |
| **Churn** | When a client stops paying / cancels the service |
| **GBP** | Google Business Profile (the Google Maps listing) |
| **SEO** | Search Engine Optimization (making websites rank higher on Google) |
| **SMM** | Social Media Management & Marketing |
| **KPI** | Key Performance Indicator (metrics that show how well someone is doing) |
| **ROI** | Return on Investment (how much money the client makes back from our service) |
| **NAP** | Name, Address, Phone — must be consistent everywhere online |
| **Scraping** | Automatically extracting data from websites using software |
| **Target Service** | The service we think this business needs most |
| **Deal Type** | The billing structure (monthly, one-time, annual, etc.) |
| **Payout** | When the company pays the team member their earned commission |
| **Rank** | A gamification tag (Rookie → Hunter → Closer → Elite → Gold → Champion) |
